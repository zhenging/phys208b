2503 Resistance

Challenge

See figure. The cross section of the resistor is circular. The radius of one end is aaa, and that of the other end is bbb, and the length of the resistor is LLL. The resistivity of the resistor is ρ\rhoρ. Find the resistance RRR.

Solution

(1) According to similarity

dd+L=ab⇒d=aLb−a\begin{aligned} \frac{d}{d + L} &= \frac{a}{b} \To d = \frac{aL}{b-a} \end{aligned} d+Ld​​=ba​⇒d=b−aaL​​

The lower bound of x is d=aLb−ad = \dfrac{aL}{b-a}d=b−aaL​, and the upper bound is d+L=bLb−ad + L = \dfrac{bL}{b-a}d+L=b−abL​
(2) The area of cross section is

xd+L=rb⇒r=(b−a)xLA=πr2=π(b−a)2x2L2\begin{aligned} \frac{x}{d + L} &= \frac{r}{b} \To r = \frac{(b-a)x}{L}\\ A &= \pi r^2 = \frac{\pi(b-a)^2 x^2}{L^2} \end{aligned} d+Lx​A​=br​⇒r=L(b−a)x​=πr2=L2π(b−a)2x2​​

(3) The resistance can be calcuated by integration.

dR=ρdxA(R=ρLA)R=∫aL/(b−a)bL/(b−a)ρπ(b−a)2x2L2dx=ρL2π(b−a)2∫aL/(b−a)bL/(b−a)dxx2=ρLπab\begin{aligned} dR &= \frac{\rho dx}{A} \quad(R = \frac{\rho L}{A})\\ R &= \int_{aL/(b-a)}^{bL/(b-a)} \frac{\rho}{\frac{\pi(b-a)^2 x^2}{L^2}} dx\\ &= \frac{\rho L^2}{\pi(b-a)^2} \int_{aL/(b-a)}^{bL/(b-a)} \frac{dx}{x^2}\\ &= \frac{\rho L}{\pi ab} \end{aligned} dRR​=Aρdx​(R=AρL​)=∫aL/(b−a)bL/(b−a)​L2π(b−a)2x2​ρ​dx=π(b−a)2ρL2​∫aL/(b−a)bL/(b−a)​x2dx​=πabρL​​
