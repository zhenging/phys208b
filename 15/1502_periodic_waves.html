1502 Periodic Waves

The wavelength λ\lambdaλ of the wave is the distance from one crest to the next, or from one trough to the next, or from any poiint ot the corresponding point on the next repetition of the wave shape. The wave pattern travels with constant speed vvv and advances a distance of one wavelength λ\lambdaλ in a time interval of one period TTT. So the wave speed is v=λ/Tv = \lambda /Tv=λ/T, or because f=1/Tf = 1/Tf=1/T.

v=λf\begin{aligned} v = \lambda f \end{aligned} v=λf​
