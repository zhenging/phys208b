3602 Diffraction From a Single Slit

Monochromatic light sent through a narrow slit of width a produces a diffraction pattern on a distant screen. Equation (36.2) gives the condition for destructive interference (a dark fringe) at a point P in the pattern at angle u. Equation (36.7) gives the intensity in the pattern as a function of u. (See Examples 36.1–36.3.)

Dark fringes, single slit diffraction

sin⁡θ=mλa(m=±1,±2,⋯)(36.2)\begin{aligned} \sin \th = \frac{m \lambda}{a} \quad (m=\pm 1, \pm 2, \cdots) \quad \bold{(36.2)} \end{aligned} sinθ=amλ​(m=±1,±2,⋯)(36.2)​

θ\thθ - Angle of line from center of slit to mth dark fringe on screen
aaa - Slit width
λ\lambdaλ - Wavelength
If the distance from slit to screen is xxx, the vertical distance of the mth dark band from the center of the patter is ymy_mym​, then tan⁡θ=ym/x\tan \th = y_m/xtanθ=ym​/x. For small θ\thθ we may also approximate tan⁡θ\tan \thtanθ by θ\thθ (in radians), We then find

ym=xmλa(ym<<x)(36.3)\begin{aligned} y_m = x\frac{m \lambda}{a} \quad (y_m << x) \quad \bold{(36.3)} \end{aligned} ym​=xamλ​(ym​<<x)(36.3)​

Examples

36.1 You pass 633-nm laser light through a narrow slit and observe the diffraction pattern on a screen 6.0 m away. The distance on the screen between the centers of the first minima on either side of the central bright fringe is 32 mm (Fig. 36.7). How wide is the slit?

Solution

The first minimum corresponds to m = 1 in Eq. (36.3). The distance y 1 from the central maximum to the first minimum on either side is half the distance between the two first minima, so y1=32/2=16y_1 = 32/2= 16y1​=32/2=16 mm. Solving Eq. (36.3) for aaa, we find

a=xλy1=0.24 mm\begin{aligned} a = x\frac{\lambda}{y_1} = 0.24\text{ mm} \end{aligned} a=xy1​λ​=0.24 mm​
