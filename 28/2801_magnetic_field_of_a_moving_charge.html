2801 Magnetic Field of a Moving Charge

Moving Charge: Vector Magnetic Field

The magnitude of B⃗\vec{B}B is proportional to ∣q∣|q|∣q∣ and to 1/r21/r^21/r2. But the direction of B⃗\vec{B}B is not along the line from source point to the field point. Instead, B⃗\vec{B}B is perpendicular to the plane containing this line and the particle's velocity vector v⃗\vec{v}v. Furthermore, the field magnitude BBB is also proportionalto the particles's speed vvv and to the sine of the angle ϕ\phiϕ.

EXAMPLE 28.1 Forces Between Two Moving Protons

Two protons move parallel to the x-axis in opposite directions (Fig. 28.2) at the same speed vvv (small compared to the speed of light ccc). At the instant shown, find the electric and magnetic forces on the upper proton and compare their magnitudes.

Solution

Coulomb's Law gives the electric force FEF_EFE​ on the upper proton. The magnetic force law gives the magnetic force on the upper proton.
The forces are replusive, so FEF_EFE​ is vertically upward (+y-direction).

FE⃗=14πϵ0q2r2j^\begin{aligned} \vec{F_E} &= \frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_0} \frac{q^2}{r^2} \jhat \end{aligned} FE​​​=4πϵ0​1​r2q2​j^​​

B⃗=μ04πq(vi^)×j^r2=μ04πqvr2k^\begin{aligned} \vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{q(v\ihat) \times \jhat}{r^2} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{qv }{r^2} \khat \end{aligned} B=4πμ0​​r2q(vi^)×j^​​=4πμ0​​r2qv​k^​
