2806 Ampere's Law

Ampere's Law is formulated not in terms of magnetic flux, but rather in terms of line integrals of B⃗\vec{B}B around a closed path, denoted by

∮B⃗⋅dl⃗\begin{aligned} \oint \vec{B}\cdot d\vec{l} \end{aligned} ∮B⋅dl​

  1. To evaluate this integral, we divide the path into infinitesimal segments dl⃗d\vec{l}dl, calculate the scalar product of B⃗⋅dl⃗\vec{B}\cdot d\vec{l}B⋅dl for each segment, and sum these products.
  2. The circle on the integral sign indicates that this integral is always computed for a closed path, one whose beginning and end points are the same.
