2401 Capacitors and Capacitance

  • Any two conductors separated by an insulator (or a vacuum) form a capacitor.
  • The ratio of charge to potential difference is called the capacitance of the capacitor.

C=QVab\begin{aligned} C = \frac{Q}{V_{ab}} \end{aligned} C=Vab​Q​​

CCC - The Capacitance of a capacitor.
QQQ - Magnitude of charge on each conductor.
VabV_{ab}Vab​ - Potential difference between conductors (a has charge +Q+Q+Q, and b has charge $-Q).

  • The SI unit of capacitance is called one farad (1F).

1F=1 farad=1C/V=1 coulomb/volt\begin{aligned} 1F = 1 \text{ farad} = 1 C/V = 1 \text{ coulomb/volt} \end{aligned} 1F=1 farad=1C/V=1 coulomb/volt​
